Kalimpong

Kalimpong is a hilltop town located at the east of river Teesta. This sleepy little town is situated at an altitude of 1,200 mtrs, some 50 kms to the east of Darjeeling. Because of its location at a relatively lower altitude, the weather is milder and pleasant throughout the year. This place is an ideal location for the tourists looking for a comfortable stay sheltered from the harsh cold winds and with a picturesque view of the Kanchenjunga and other Himalayan snow peaks. Kalimpong is a melting pot of diverse cultures which is reflected in its cuisines, architecture, and amongst the locals


Visit this town for its stunning mountain views, quaint walks across the town while having a bite or two in the local eateries. Amongst the activities around Kalimpong, one can indulge in white water rafting along the river Teesta, hiking, trekking, village walk etc. These experiences are enhanced by the magnificent view of the surrounding hills, and riverbanks.

By Air: The nearest airport to Kalimpong is Bagdogra. There are direct flight connections with Delhi, Kolkata, Howrah, and Guwahati. There are several transport facilities available from Bagdogra to reach Kalimpong.

By Train: The two closest railway stations are Siliguri and New Jalpaiguri. These railway stations have direct railway connections with Kolkata, Delhi, Guwahati, Varanasi, and other major cities of India.

By Road:The major access to Kalimpong by road is via Siliguri, which is connected to all the major cities.

Army Golf Club: Located among the hilly terrains of Durpin Dara, lies the 18 hole Army golf course maintained by the Indian Army at Kalimpong in West Bengal. Army officers can be seen playing golf in the early hours of morning. The place has special significance for tourists as the surrounding areas are covered with trees such as Oaks, Dhupi (Cryptomeria Japonica) and Cypress which increases the splendor of the place. Tourists can also witness the majestic Mt. Kanchenjunga visible in the backdrop. Nature lovers will surely love this place because of the surreal charm of its surrounding which is completely unique in its own ways.


Army Golf Club:Lava is a small hamlet town about 33km away from Kalimpong and is surrounded by pine forests and is often engulfed in mists and clouds. It is situated at an altitude of 2,350 meters. The village hosts a beautiful monastery of Bhutanese origin and a Nature Interpretation Centre. This place is popular amongst nature explorers and is renowned for bird watching. It is also the starting point for treks into the Neora Valley National Park, which abounds with floral and faunal wealth. The route to Lava is scenic with the change in vegetation from tropical deciduous to the wet alpine trees of fir, pine and birch. The lush forests of Lava are ideal for nature treks, bird watching, and plant and wildlife exploration. The Sherpa View Point here provides an excellent view of the snow-crested mountains of Bhutan and Tibet.


Rachela Pass:Rachela Pass is a 12km trekking route from Lava through the Neora Valley Nature Reserve. The route being steep offers scenic beauty of magnolias, rhododendrons, and bamboo trees. The treks highest point is the Rachela top on the ridge forming the border. It’s a tri junction between Sikkim, West Bengal, and Bhutan. The region is also a natural habitat for Red Panda and Tigers.


Lolegaon: A small peaceful village situated at the far end of the Himalayan Ridge and is popular for its beautiful landscapes, thriving forests and serene views of the Kanchenjunga. This place will surely awaken the true explorer in you, from the casual strolls amidst the dense jungle, to the walks on the hanging bridge (also known as Canopy Walk) in the middle of the forest of oak, fir, birch, and cypress trees which gives a view of the forest from an altitude. Lolegaon offers several small treks and trails for adventure seeking tourists. It is an ideal destination for unwinding & relaxation away from the hustle and bustle of city life.


Changey Waterfalls:Changey Waterfalls is one of the must visit exotic locations to visit in Lava. Waterfall amidst the hills and forests, cascading down from a height of about 150 feet makes this place mesmerizing . Exotic birds such as White Capped Water Redstart, Verditer Flycatcher, Niltava, Blue Fronted Redstart, Grey Treepie, Indian Robin and Blue Whistling Thrush can be spotted at this location, making the experience a lot more enchanting.


Neora Valley National Park:Neora Valley National Park is considered as one of the most endowed biological zones, which spreads over an area of about 88 Sq. km. and is situated near Lava in the foothill of majestic Himalayan Mountains. This park is bordered by the forests of Sikkim on one side and by Bhutan on the other. Wildlife enthusiasts can enjoy visiting this place as it houses different varieties of animals and birds. Tourists can easily sight the golden headed black finch, blind snake, and king cobra in their natural habitat. This valley is known for its picnic spot where children and adults can enjoy.


Lolegaon:The meaning of the word Rishop is ‘mountain peak and tree’ and it is located between the junctions of Sikkim, West Bengal, and Bhutan. Rishop's unique feature is that there is only empty space between this tiny hamlet and the Kangchenjunga range standing on the horizon. Rishop offers breathtaking view of the majestic Kangchenjunga and various mountain peak of India and Nepal. This small village houses a lot of homestays which will provide comfortable stay and from there one can look at the mighty Kanchenjunga or at night the view of the lights of the small villages on the hill.
